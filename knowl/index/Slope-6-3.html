Activity 3.9. Negative Slopes.

Increasing and Decreasing.
An increasing quantity has a positive rate of change, or slope, and a decreasing quantity has a negative rate of change.
Horizontal and Vertical Changes.
  • When we move to the right on a graph, \(\Delta x\) is positive.
  • When we move to the left, \(\Delta x\) is negative.
  • When we move up on a graph, \(\Delta y\) is positive.
  • When we move down, \(\Delta y\) is negative.
  1. The value of new office equipment decreases, or depreciates, over time. The graph shows the value, \(V\text{,}\) in thousands of dollars, of a large copy machine \(t\) years after it was purchased.
    1. Compute the slope of the line by moving from point \(A\) to point \(B\text{.}\)
    2. What does the slope tell you about the value of the machine?
    3. Compute the slope of the line by moving from point \(B\) to point \(A\text{.}\)
  2. Find the slope of each line segment. Verify that you get the same answer if you move in the opposite direction. (Each square counts for one unit.)
