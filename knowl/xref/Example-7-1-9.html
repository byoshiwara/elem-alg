Example 7.13.

Subtract the polynomials \(~(4x^2+2x-5)-(2x^2-3x-2)\)

Solution.

We first remove the parentheses, remembering to change the sign of each term of the subtracted polynomial.
\begin{align*} (4x^2 \amp +2x-5)-(2x^2-3x-2) \amp \amp \blert{\text{Change signs.}}\\ \amp = 4x^2+2x-5 \blert{-} 2x^2\blert{+}3x\blert{+}2\amp \amp \blert{\text{Combine like terms.}}\\ \amp = 2x^2+5x-3 \end{align*}
