Example 5.11.

Compare the expressions \(2x^3\) and \((2x)^3\text{.}\)

Solution.

In the first expression, only the \(x\) is cubed. Thus,
\begin{align*} 2x^3~~~~ \amp \text{means}~~~~2xxx \amp \amp \blert{\text{Exponent applies to the base,}~x~\text{only.}}\\ (2x)^3~~~~\amp \text{means}~~~~(2x)(2x)(2x) \amp \amp \blert{\text{Exponent applies to the base,}~2x.} \end{align*}
The two expressions are not equivalent, as you can see by evaluating each for, say, \(x=\alert{5}\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} 2x^3 \amp =2(\alert{5}^3) = 2(125) = 250\\ (2x)^3\amp =(2 \cdot \alert{5})^3 = 10^3 = 1000 \end{align*}
